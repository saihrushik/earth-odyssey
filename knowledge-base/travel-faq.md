# Travel FAQ

**When is the cheapest time to book flights?** For international trips, 2–4 months before departure is the statistical sweet spot; for peak season (December holidays, August in Europe) book 5–8 months out. Tuesday–Wednesday departures average 10–20% cheaper than Friday–Sunday. Fare alerts beat refreshing: prices move several times a week.

**Do I need travel insurance?** For international trips, yes — medical evacuation alone can cost tens of thousands. Look for coverage of emergency medical (≥$100k), trip interruption, and adventure activities if you plan to hike, dive, or ski. Many premium credit cards include baseline trip protection.

**How do I beat jet lag?** Shift your sleep 1 hour per day toward the destination timezone for 3 days before flying east; get morning light on arrival. Flying west is easier — stay up until local night. Hydrate on the plane and skip alcohol; caffeine only before 2pm local.

**What's the safest way to carry money abroad?** A no-foreign-fee card as primary, a second card in a different bag, and one day of local cash. Airport ATMs from real banks beat currency exchange counters everywhere. Decline "convert to home currency" at terminals — dynamic currency conversion adds 3–8%.

**When do I need a visa?** Check the destination's official immigration site 8+ weeks before travel — third-party summaries go stale. E-visas (India, Türkiye, Kenya) usually clear in days; embassy visas (China outside transit exemptions, some of Africa) can take weeks. Many countries require 6 months of passport validity beyond your travel dates and proof of onward travel.

**How much should I tip?** USA: 18–20% at restaurants. Japan: never — it can read as rude. Europe: round up or 5–10%. Middle East/North Africa: small notes for nearly every service. When unsure, ask your first taxi driver — locals give the straight answer.

**eSIM or local SIM?** For trips under a month, an eSIM (activated before landing) wins on convenience; for longer stays a local SIM is cheaper per GB. Either way, download offline maps of your first city before wheels-down.
